# Character Balance Guide: Generalissimo vs Demagogue

## Overview

The two playable characters represent opposite fighting archetypes designed to create interesting matchups and different playstyles.

---

## Character Archetypes

### Generalissimo (Character 1)
- **Archetype**: Tank / Power Hitter
- **Playstyle**: Aggressive pressure with strong single hits
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-friendly (forgiving health pool)
- **Philosophy**: "Win through superior strength"

### Demagogue (Character 2)
- **Archetype**: Glass Cannon / Hit-and-Run
- **Playstyle**: Rapid evasion with consistent chip damage
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate (requires active spacing)
- **Philosophy**: "Win through superior speed and pressure"

## Gameplay Dynamics

### Round Opening (Neutral)
**Generalissimo Advantage**:
- Higher damage threat keeps Demagogue defensive
- Can slowly advance with health advantage

**Demagogue Advantage**:
- Faster startup beats slower moves
- Superior speed creates unpredictable angles of attack

**Neutral Play**: Demagogue should use superior speed to control spacing and avoid trades.

### Mid-Round (Pressure)
**Generalissimo Advantage**:
- Stronger hits force bigger knockback on Demagogue
- Heavier weight means less knockback vulnerability
- Can trade hits favorably due to higher HP

**Demagogue Advantage**:
- Faster light attack startup (2f vs 4f) allows constant poke
- Speed boost special allows escaping corner
- Lighter weight for mobility (but higher knockback risk)

**Pressure Play**: Generalissimo presses forward, Demagogue looks for gaps to counter.

### Endgame (Low Health)
**Generalissimo Advantage**:
- 85 HP Demagogue dies to fewer hits
- 100 HP Generalissimo can afford more mistakes
- Safer to trade when ahead

**Demagogue Advantage**:
- If trailing on health, higher speed allows last-second miraculous escapes
- Faster startup means last-minute reversal attempts
- Special move's speed boost extends desperation combos

**Endgame Play**: Generalissimo plays safe with health lead, Demagogue gets aggressive.

## Team Building Implications

### Generalissimo Strengths
1. Forgiving health pool for new players
2. High damage per hit (KO threat)
3. Strong knockback for combo breaking
4. Heavy weight makes spacing less critical

### Generalissimo Weaknesses
1. Slower startup means reacting is harder
2. Heavy weight limits mobility
3. Losing to a faster character is frustrating
4. Less comeback potential

### Demagogue Strengths
1. Fast startup enables reactive gameplay
2. Superior mobility for spacing control
3. Speed boost special for momentum shifts
4. Rewarding execution-based playstyle

### Demagogue Weaknesses
1. Lower health pool = less forgiving
2. Takes more knockback = spacing critical
3. Lower damage means grinding wins
4. Steeper learning curve

---

## Balance Validation

### Is Generalissimo Overpowered?
**No** - Demagogue's speed advantage compensates:
- 2f light attack startup beats Generalissimo's slower moves
- 22% speed increase enables better positioning
- Special move's utility (speed boost) adds depth

### Is Demagogue Underpowered?
**No** - Differences are intentional and balanced:
- Health: -15% offset by +22% speed
- Weight: -33% offset by more evasion options
- Damage: -20% offset by faster hitrate potential

### Skill Floor vs Skill Ceiling
- **Generalissimo**: Lower skill floor (easier to learn), medium ceiling
- **Demagogue**: Higher skill floor (needs spacing), higher ceiling (more nuance)

## Future Balance Adjustments

If testing reveals imbalances:

### If Generalissimo Too Strong
- Reduce heavy attack damage (15 → 13)
- Reduce health slightly (100 → 95)
- Increase light attack startup (4 → 5 frames)

### If Demagogue Too Weak
- Increase health (85 → 90)
- Increase light attack damage (6 → 7)
- Reduce speed weight penalty (0.8 → 0.85)

### If Special Moves Unbalanced
- Adjust Rally Cry damage or effect timing
- Balance unknown Generalissimo special
- Ensure both have utility + damage

---

## Conclusion

The Generalissimo and Demagogue represent a classic fighting game archetype pairing:
- **Tank vs Agile** (Street Fighter: Zangief vs Cammy)
- **Power vs Speed** (Tekken: King vs Yoshimitsu)
- **Heavy vs Light** (Soul Calibur: Astaroth vs Voldo)

This design encourages:
1. Different playstyles with equal viability
2. Interesting matchups with distinct strategies
3. Skill expression through execution
4. Clear character identity and fantasy

The balance should emerge naturally through testing and tournament play.
